⏱ Evolution: from RNN to BERT
Pre-2017 · RNN / LSTM
Words processed one by one, sequentially. Models read left-to-right or right-to-left only. Struggled to remember context over long sequences.
2017 · Transformer
Processes all words in parallel (faster training). Introduced self-attention — every word can look at every other word simultaneously.
2018 · BERT
Uses the Transformer's encoder only. Reads context from both left and right simultaneously — true bidirectional understanding.
↔️ What does "Bidirectional" mean?
Key idea: To understand the meaning of a word, BERT reads words before it (left context) and words after it (right context) at the same time.
← Left context Target word Right context →
⚖️ Old models vs BERT

⚠ Traditional Models (RNN/LSTM)

  • Read text in one direction only
  • Left-to-right OR right-to-left
  • Miss the complete context
  • Struggle with ambiguous words
  • Slow sequential processing

✦ BERT

  • Reads left AND right simultaneously
  • True bidirectional understanding
  • Captures full word context
  • Resolves ambiguity from context
  • Parallel processing (faster)
🎭 How BERT Learns – Masked Language Model (MLM)
MLM: During training, BERT randomly hides (masks) some words. It must predict the missing word using surrounding context — from both directions.
