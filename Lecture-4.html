Part 01
Why Outsource to a Machine?

Imagine reading a 500-page technical document. A human might take 10 days. A machine can process it in 10 minutes. That's the core motivation behind modern AI.

📚
The 500-Page Problem
Large volumes of text are time-consuming for humans to read, understand, and summarise. AI systems can ingest and process them in seconds.
⚡
Speed × Scale
10 days → 10 minutes. AI doesn't get tired, doesn't need breaks, and scales instantly — processing thousands of documents simultaneously.
🤖
Outsourcing Intelligence
We delegate repetitive, pattern-heavy cognitive work to software. The machine learns how to do it — we define the goal.
Part 02
The AI Timeline

Key milestones that shaped modern Natural Language Processing.

Pre-2017
Rule-Based & Statistical Models
Hardcoded logic, no learning ability. Later, statistical models introduced probability rules. All followed rigid predefined patterns (e.g., Ajax-based autocomplete).
2013
Word2Vec
Revolutionised NLP by capturing semantic meaning in words — "vacation," "travel," "hotel" cluster together because they share contextual meaning.
Pre-2017
RNNs & LSTMs
Recurrent Neural Networks processed words one by one. LSTMs (Long Short-Term Memory) and GRUs were designed to handle longer dependencies — but still had limits.
2017 ⭐
Transformers — "Attention Is All You Need"
The landmark paper introduced the Transformer architecture, replacing RNNs entirely. All words are processed simultaneously (parallel processing), solving long-dependency issues and dramatically speeding up training.
2018
BERT
Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers — reads text in both directions for deeper language understanding. Google's milestone NLP model.
2020+
Rise of Large Language Models (LLMs)
GPT-3, GPT-4, T5, Gemini and others emerged — capable of text generation, code writing, image understanding and even multimodal (text + image) reasoning.
2025
GPT-4 & Multimodal AI
Multimodal understanding — text, images, audio, video — in a single model. Text-to-video generation becomes mainstream. Agentic AI models that take decisions autonomously.
Part 03
RNN / LSTM vs Transformer

Why did Transformers replace the previous generation of sequence models?

Aspect RNN / LSTM Transformer
Processing Style Word-by-word (sequential) Slow All words at once (parallel) Fast
Long Dependencies Struggles to remember distant context Weak Attention mechanism handles any distance Strong
Training Speed Slower due to sequential steps Significantly faster with parallelism
Example Problem "Kohli didn't play because he was not fit" — who is "he"? Hard after many words. Attention directly links "he" back to "Kohli" regardless of distance.
Modern Usage Mostly replaced / legacy Foundation of all modern LLMs

Part 05
Generative vs Discriminative AI

Two fundamentally different approaches that serve complementary purposes.

🎨 Generative AI

Creates New Data

Generates new outcomes — text, images, music, code — that did not exist before.

  • Images from descriptions ("a cat on the beach")
  • Text generation (stories, summaries, Q&A)
  • Code synthesis
  • ⚠️ Output may be inaccurate, misleading, or offensive — always verify!
🔍 Discriminative AI

Classifies Existing Data

Analyses and classifies the data that already exists — makes predictions and decisions.

  • Diabetic / Non-diabetic classification
  • Win / Lose election prediction
  • Fake image detector (real vs AI-generated)
  • Text sentiment classification

💡 Key insight from the lecture: At the back-end, even Generative AI systems use Discriminative models for evaluation during training (e.g., GANs — Generative Adversarial Networks — use a Discriminator to judge if generated images are "real enough").

Part 07
Next-Word Prediction

Before Transformers, search engines used predefined Ajax-based autocomplete. Transformers replaced this with learned next-word prediction based on context — understanding partial queries, spelling mistakes, and semantic intent.

🕰️
Old: Ajax Autocomplete
Each character you type triggers a JavaScript call returning a predefined pattern list. "What is m…" → hardcoded: machine learning, mobile net, etc. No real understanding.
🧠
New: Transformer Prediction
The model truly understands context. It handles spelling mistakes ("lapy" → laptop), partial words ("ML" → machine learning), and semantic synonyms.
🔗
Semantic Word Clusters
Type "vacation" → the model surfaces related words: travel, hotel, trip, flights, April, holiday, transport — because it understands meaning, not just spelling.
Part 08
Pre-Trained Models

Instead of training from scratch, we reuse powerful models already trained on enormous datasets. We only add a small task-specific layer on top.

🖼️
CNN (Image) Pre-Trained Models
ResNet-50, MobileNet-V2, EfficientNet — available via Keras / TensorFlow. We load the weights and attach a classification head for our specific task (e.g., flower recognition).
📝
NLP Pre-Trained Models
BERT, GPT, T5, Gemini — trained on billions of text tokens. Fine-tune on your domain with a fraction of the compute and data.
🎵
Speech Pre-Trained Models
WaveNet and similar — trained on large audio corpora. Both generative (speech synthesis) and discriminative (speech-to-text) variants exist.
🚀

The Big Picture

The Transformer architecture (2017) solved the fundamental problems of sequential processing and long-range dependencies that plagued RNNs. By processing all tokens in parallel and using attention to weigh relationships between any two words — regardless of distance — Transformers became the backbone of every major modern AI system, from ChatGPT to Gemini to DALL·E.
